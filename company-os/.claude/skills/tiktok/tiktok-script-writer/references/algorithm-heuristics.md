# TikTok Algorithm Heuristics (2026)

Verified posting rules for TikTok's For You Page algorithm as of 2025-2026.

---

## Signal hierarchy (what TikTok rewards)

In order of weight:

1. **Completion rate** — Did people watch to the end? This is the #1 signal. A video with 80% completion rate will be pushed aggressively, even if likes and comments are low.
2. **Shares** — The strongest distribution signal. Shared to DMs or other apps = TikTok treats it as high-value.
3. **Replays** — Watched again? Very high satisfaction signal.
4. **Comments** — Especially creator replies within the first 2 hours.
5. **Likes** — Weakest signal. High likes with low completion = pretty but not engaging.
6. **Follows from the video** — Indicates the content converted a new fan.

**Implication:** A video that 80% of people watch to the end is more valuable than a video with 100k likes and 30% completion. Optimize for completion, not vanity metrics.

---

## The For You Page (FYP) mechanism

TikTok recommends based on **content signals**, not account signals. This is the fundamental difference from Instagram and LinkedIn.

**What this means:**
- A new account with 50 followers can go viral immediately if completion rate is high
- A 100k-follower account with low completion videos will struggle
- The algorithm continuously tests content with new audiences — if it performs, it expands

**Testing cascade:**
1. Small seed audience (~100-500 viewers)
2. If completion + shares are strong → expanded to similar interest profiles (~10k)
3. Strong again → broader topic-based audience (~100k+)
4. Strong again → For You Page at scale

The first 200-500 views are the test. If those viewers finish the video, TikTok keeps pushing.

## Completion rate benchmarks (2025-2026)

| Completion Rate | FYP Treatment |
|---|---|
| 80%+ | Strong push to wider audiences |
| 50-79% | Moderate push |
| 30-49% | Neutral — minimal expansion |
| Below 30% | Deprioritized; rarely expanded |

**Tip:** Shorter videos have higher completion rates by default. A 15-second video with 70% completion rate often outperforms a 60-second video with 70% completion (because the 60-second one implies higher absolute watch time but the same percentage).

## The first 3 seconds

**80% of viewers decide in the first 3 seconds** whether to stay or swipe.

TikTok's algorithm also uses the first-3-second retention as a signal separate from overall completion. A video that holds 90% of viewers at 3 seconds but drops to 40% completion is still weighted differently from one that holds 60% at 3 seconds and reaches 70% completion.

**Implications:**
- The hook is the most important script investment
- Visual action in second 1 (something happening on screen) holds viewers better than a talking head with no visual movement
- On-screen text visible within the first 2 seconds holds muted viewers

---

## Trending sounds

Using trending audio gives a **~20-40% initial reach boost** because TikTok's algorithm groups videos by sound and shows them together.

**Rules for trending sounds:**
- Use trending sounds for Reach/Trend pillar content
- For tutorial or educational content, original audio is fine (and often better for authority)
- Don't force a trending sound onto content where it doesn't fit — poor retention cancels the reach boost
- Check the "Creator Sound" tab or trending sound lists weekly (sounds trend and die fast)

|---|---|
| 6:00–8:00 AM local | Early risers, commuters |
| 12:00–2:00 PM local | Lunch scroll |
| 7:00–9:00 PM local | Peak evening session (highest volume) |
| 9:00–11:00 PM local | Secondary evening peak |

**B2B/professional audience:** 7-8 AM and 12-1 PM.
**Consumer/entertainment:** 7-11 PM.

## Posting cadence

| Account type | Optimal videos/day |
|---|---|
| Growth sprint | 1-3 videos/day |
| Maintenance | 1 video/day |
| Brand account | 1 video/day |

**More than 3/day:** Possible but quality degrades. Each additional video cannibalizes the previous one's early engagement window. Most creators cap at 2-3/day during growth periods.

**Consistency > frequency:** An account posting 1 video/day for 30 days outperforms an account posting 5/day for 6 days then nothing.

---

## Comments and creator replies

**Reply to ALL comments within 2 hours of posting.** Creator replies in this window significantly boost For You Page distribution (estimated 15-25% reach lift, similar to YouTube's 2-hour comment window).

**Video replies:** TikTok allows video replies to comments. These are boosted by the algorithm. If a comment asks a question, a 15-30 second video reply reaches both your audience and often the commenter's followers.

---

## Duets and Stitches

Both formats are **strongly boosted** by TikTok's algorithm because they:
1. Drive more watch time (combined content)
2. Create conversation signals
3. Introduce your content to the original creator's audience

**Duet rules:**
- Add genuine commentary — not just "this is great!"
- Your side of the duet should be independently interesting (someone who hasn't seen the original still finds value)

**Stitch rules:**
- Take a specific clip and add your perspective
- Works best when you agree-and-extend or respectfully disagree
- Stitch window: first 5-10 seconds of the original video

---

## Never delete videos

Old TikTok videos can go viral weeks or months after posting — the algorithm continues testing them with new audiences. Deleting a video removes that opportunity permanently.

**Exception:** Factually incorrect information or content that violates guidelines.

---

## What NOT to do

- **Buying followers or engagement:** TikTok detects inauthentic engagement and suppresses accounts
- **Posting only when inspired:** The algorithm rewards consistency; irregular posting loses the FYP momentum
- **Ignoring comments:** The 2-hour reply window is a concrete reach multiplier
- **Long intros:** Every second before the hook is a second of viewer loss
- **Same format every video:** TikTok's algorithm tests content diversity; monotony signals low effort
- **Private account during growth:** Private accounts can't be recommended on For You Page
