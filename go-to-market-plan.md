## Executive Summary

CompanyOS is an AI operating system built on Claude Code: a second brain (PARA method + persistent memory) plus a humanized content engine (70+ skills across LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok, X, YouTube, newsletters, ads, documents). It replaces a content team, a paid media specialist, and a scattered tool stack for people who don't have (or don't want) those teams.

The GTM strategy is **open-core + build-in-public**: the core repo stays free on GitHub (the democratization angle *is* the acquisition engine), and revenue comes from setup services, a paid membership with premium skill packs, and an agency/white-label license. Distribution is founder-led content on LinkedIn/X/YouTube — created *with* CompanyOS itself, which is the proof of the product.

**90-Day Goals** (assumptions — adjust to your capacity):
- 1,000 GitHub stars / installs of the free core
- 50 paying customers across tiers
- $5K MRR-equivalent (mix of one-time + recurring)
- Key milestone: 10 documented case studies ("what one person shipped in a week with CompanyOS")

## 1. Target Customer & Beachhead Market

**Personas** (from README, ranked by ease of close):

1. **Solopreneur / creator** — publishes constantly, drowning in Canva and scheduling. Buys fast, low ticket. *Beachhead.*
2. **Freelancer / consultant** — needs proposals, deliverables, client emails. Values time saved per project.
3. **Agency owner** — multi-client content pipelines. Highest willingness to pay; needs the white-label license.
4. **SMB CEO** — wants a "second brain" for the whole company: memory, strategy, reports, internal docs. Longest sales cycle; sold via case studies, not ads.

**Beachhead**: solo entrepreneurs and creators already curious about AI who post on LinkedIn/X at least weekly. They feel the pain daily, they're reachable organically, and they become the public case studies that later sell agencies and CEOs.

**Buying journey**: sees a "built with CompanyOS" post → visits repo/landing page → installs free core (10 min) → hits the "I want this configured properly" moment → buys setup or membership. Timeline: 1–14 days.

## 2. Positioning & Messaging

**Positioning statement**:
For solo entrepreneurs, CEOs, and lean teams who need a second brain and a content engine but can't hire one, CompanyOS is an AI operating system that knows your business, writes like you, and gets smarter every session — unlike ChatGPT prompts or SaaS point tools that forget you the moment you close the tab.

**Key messages**:
1. **"It knows you."** Persistent memory + PARA second brain. Not prompts — an operating system that compounds.
2. **"Humanized, not AI-flavored."** Your tone, your examples, your anti-patterns captured once in a 5-minute interview. The humanizer skill is a headline feature, not a footnote.
3. **"One person, whole team output."** Content team + media buyer + document factory in one folder.
4. **Democratization**: "The core is free forever. You pay for speed, not access."

## 5. Second Revenue Stream (your requested extra option)

**Recommended: Certified Operator program (services marketplace).**
Train freelancers/VAs to implement CompanyOS for clients who don't want to DIY (CEOs, SMBs — exactly the segment that won't clone a repo). Charge for certification ($200–400), then route inbound "do it for me" leads to operators for a 15–20% referral fee. This monetizes the audience you *can't* convert to self-serve, creates an army of advocates selling on your behalf, and scales without your hours.

**Runner-ups** (viable later): productized done-for-you retainer ($500–1,500/mo per client — highest revenue per customer but consumes founder time); paid cohort course "Run your company on AI in 14 days" ($197–497, launch quarterly); vertical skill packs sold à la carte (real estate, clinics, e-commerce).

## 7. Success Metrics

**Weekly**: repo installs/stars, landing visits, community joins, free→paid conversion, sales by tier.
**Monthly**: MRR + one-time revenue, churn on membership, CAC by channel (time-cost included), case studies published.

Leading indicator to obsess over: **installs → Setup Sprint conversion**. If it's under 2%, the free tier is too complete or the paid offer is unclear — fix the offer before touching channels.

## 8. Risks & Mitigation

1. **"It's free on GitHub, why pay?"** — Sell outcome and speed, not files: setup done-with-you, ongoing packs, community. Open-core companies (Cal.com, Plausible) prove this works.
2. **Platform dependency on Claude Code** (pricing/behavior changes) — keep skills as portable markdown; position as "AI-OS" not "Claude add-on"; document costs transparently for buyers.
3. **Founder time is the whole engine** — content pipeline runs on CompanyOS itself (2h/day cap); Certified Operators absorb service demand by Month 3.
4. **Copycats forking the repo** — moat is memory architecture + community + your public track record, not the files. Ship weekly.
5. **Wrong beachhead** — if creators don't convert but agencies do, invert the ladder (lead with Agency tier). Review at Day 45.
