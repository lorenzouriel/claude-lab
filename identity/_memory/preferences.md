# Preferences

> Voice, tone, and style. Skills read this before generating any content.

## Voice analysis

From studying real writing samples (LinkedIn posts in PT-BR, Medium articles in EN):

- **Sentence length:** Short and punchy on social (1-2 sentences per paragraph). Medium-length in tutorials. Mix of both in reflective pieces.
- **Formality:** Conversational-professional. Direct, never corporate. Sounds like a practitioner talking, not a consultant presenting.
- **Person:** First-person. "I built", "I decided", "I was 23 years old."
- **Energy:** Confident and direct. States things without hedging. Opens with the problem, ends with the solution or CTA.
- **Lists:** Uses → arrows, not bullet points. Numbers are always specific (not "about 15 minutes" — "15 minutos").
- **Structure:** Problem → Solution → CTA. Every post has a clear reason to exist.
- **Technical content:** Code blocks, folder trees, and diagrams are common. Explains the "why" before the "how".
- **Language:** Bilingual. LinkedIn and Instagram in PT-BR. Medium and Substack in EN. Generate in the appropriate language based on the platform.

## Writing example

LinkedIn (PT-BR):
> "Eu montei o Data Eyes pra acabar com aquela bagunça de scripts espalhados. Quem é DBA sabe do trabalho."

Medium (EN):
> "Communication is always the companies nightmare, every customers and project that I worked on I see this problem. I couldn't do anything… until I decided to do something."

Medium personal (EN):
> "My first contact with the technology field happened at 15 years old, when I started a technical IT course to complement school."

## Always avoid

- Anything that reads like AI-generated text — always run content through the /humanizer skill before delivering
- Emojis (except occasional ☕ in coffee CTAs and similar personal content)
- Corporate jargon and buzzwords (synergy, leverage, impactful, stakeholder alignment)
- Vague hedging ("it might be worth considering", "this could potentially")
- Bullet points with bold headers followed by a colon — use flowing text or → arrows instead
- Em dashes — use commas, periods, or colons instead
- Rule of three constructions
- Generic positive conclusions ("the future looks bright")
- "Let me know if you have questions" or any chatbot sign-off language

## Style notes

- Opens posts with a problem statement or an observation, not an introduction
- Ends with a CTA: GitHub link, coffee link, invite to connect, question to the reader
- Code is clean, PascalCase for tables, snake_case for columns
- Comments in code explain WHY, not WHAT
- Personal stories are specific: real dates, real company names, real numbers
- Never rounds numbers or softens claims

## Humanizer

Before delivering any content piece, apply the /humanizer skill to remove AI writing patterns. The voice samples above are the calibration reference.
