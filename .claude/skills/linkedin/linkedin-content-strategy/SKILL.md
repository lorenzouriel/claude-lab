# LinkedIn Content Strategy

Build a LinkedIn presence that attracts the right people, positions you as the authority in your space, and generates inbound — without sounding like a corporate press release or a motivational poster.

**Core principle:** LinkedIn success = Sharp expertise × Earned trust × Comment engine, compounding over 6–12 months.

## When to Use

- Building a LinkedIn presence from scratch or after a long absence
- Diagnosing why impressions, followers, or inbound have stalled
- Planning a 30–90 day content system for a growth sprint
- Positioning as an authority in a B2B niche
- Generating leads, job offers, partnerships, or speaking opportunities from LinkedIn

---

## What LinkedIn Actually Is

LinkedIn is not Twitter with a suit on. It is not a job board. It is not a polished press release machine. It is the only platform where professional credibility is the primary currency — and where the audience is in a buying mindset, a hiring mindset, or a learning mindset by default.

| Platform | Primary currency | Winning quality | Audience state |
|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok | Entertainment | Fast + relatable | Passive, browsing |
| Instagram | Aesthetics + trust | Beautiful + authentic | Passive, inspired |
| X | Opinions + speed | Sharp + immediate | Active, engaged |
| **LinkedIn** | **Professional credibility** | **Insightful + human** | **Active, professional intent** |

The LinkedIn audience is willing to stop and read. They will spend 2–3 minutes on a carousel or a long text post — something they would never do on TikTok or X. The platform rewards depth and specificity in ways no other social platform does.

## How LinkedIn's Algorithm Works

Understanding the algorithm means understanding why some posts explode and others disappear.

**The dwell time signal.** LinkedIn measures how long someone pauses on your post — not just whether they liked it. A post that makes people stop and read for 30 seconds beats a post that gets 100 likes in 5 seconds. This is why carousels and long-form text posts outperform image posts with short captions.

**The "See more" cutoff.** LinkedIn truncates text posts at ~210 characters. Everything after that requires a click. The first 210 characters determine whether 90% of your audience reads the rest. This is your effective hook limit.

**The early engagement window.** The first 60–90 minutes after posting determine long-term reach. LinkedIn shows your post to a small sample of your network first. If they engage (comments especially), LinkedIn expands distribution to their networks, then further. If they scroll past, the post dies quietly.

**Comments > everything else.** LinkedIn's algorithm weights comments at roughly 8–10x the weight of likes. A post with 10 comments reaches 3–4x more people than a post with 100 likes and 0 comments. Comments also extend the post's lifespan — a post can continue gaining reach for 48–72 hours if comments keep coming in.

**External links are penalized.** Like X, LinkedIn's algorithm deprioritizes posts with external URLs. Put links in the first comment, not the post body. Exception: LinkedIn newsletters and LinkedIn articles (native content — no penalty).

**Connection vs. follower reach.** Your first-degree connections see your content first. Their networks see it if they engage. Followers (non-connections who follow you) receive your content after connections. This means your connection list quality matters more than its size.

## LinkedIn's Content Formats

### Text Posts — The Primary Format

Plain text with no image or link. The highest-performing format on LinkedIn by reach-to-effort ratio. LinkedIn's algorithm slightly favors native text over images because dwell time is naturally higher.

**Optimal length:** 900–1,300 characters for maximum reach. Under 500 characters rarely performs. Over 2,000 characters loses readers (though exceptions exist for high-authority accounts).

**When to use:**
- Opinions and hot takes
- Confessions and lessons from mistakes
- Behind-the-scenes of your work
- Data-driven insights from your experience
- Story-driven posts (the most powerful format on LinkedIn)

---

### Document/PDF Carousels — The Save Magnet

LinkedIn's native carousel format. Upload a PDF and LinkedIn displays it as a swipeable deck. This is the format that earns the most saves and reposts.

**Why it works:** Each "slide turn" is a new dwell event. LinkedIn counts every page turn as engagement. A 10-slide carousel where 60% of viewers make it to the end generates more algorithm signal than most other formats.

**Optimal length:** 7–12 slides. Under 5 = not enough value. Over 15 = people abandon mid-deck.

**Cover slide (the only slide most people see):**
- Bold, high-contrast text — readable at thumbnail size
- One promise: what will they know after swiping?
- 5–7 words maximum

**Internal slides:**
- One insight per slide
- Short text (people swipe, they don't read paragraphs)
- Visual hierarchy: a number or headline > supporting text

**Last slide:**
- One CTA only
- Your handle (@yourname) so screenshots are attributed
- What to do next (save, DM, link in first comment)

---

### Polls — The Engagement Spike

Polls generate immediate engagement (votes) and often provoke comments ("I chose X because..."). They're the fastest path to a high-comment post.

**Best uses:**
- Market research on your audience's real opinions
- Revealing a counterintuitive result ("I thought X would win. Here's what actually happened:")
- Positioning a product/service tension without selling it

**Cadence:** Max 1 per week. Overuse kills credibility — they start to look like engagement bait.

**Follow up always.** Post the result + your take as a comment or a follow-up post. The loop closes the conversation and extends the post's lifespan.

---

### LinkedIn Newsletter — The Retention Engine

A LinkedIn newsletter is a long-form article series that subscribers opt into. When you publish an issue, every subscriber gets an in-app notification AND an email. Unlike posts, newsletter reach doesn't depend on the first-hour algorithm — it goes to every subscriber directly.

**Why it's underused and powerful:**
- Your subscribers chose to receive it (higher intent than followers)
- Direct notification delivery = no algorithm gate
- LinkedIn rewards newsletter publishers with extra reach on their regular posts
- Articles are indexed by LinkedIn search (free SEO)

**What to publish:**
- Weekly or bi-weekly issue on your core topic
- Longer-form analysis you can't fit in a post (1,000–3,000 words)
- Curated insights from your work with one clear takeaway

**Activation strategy:** When you publish a newsletter issue, also post about it as a regular post to drive subscriptions. Cross-pollinate: your posts attract followers → followers become newsletter subscribers → subscribers are your highest-intent audience.

---

### Video — The Trust Accelerator

Video on LinkedIn is still underused relative to its reach potential. LinkedIn boosts native video because they want to compete with YouTube and TikTok for video consumption. The bar is low — most LinkedIn video is low-quality, which means well-produced (or authentically raw) video stands out easily.

**Formats that work:**
- Talking-head (1–3 minutes): your take on a trend, a lesson, a case study
- Screen recording: tutorial, tool walkthrough, data analysis
- Behind-the-scenes: show the work, not just the result

**What doesn't work:**
- Polished corporate video (feels like an ad)
- Vague motivational content
- Talking head with no structure (rambling kills completion rate)

**Caption rule:** Always add subtitles. 70%+ of LinkedIn video is watched muted.

## The 3-Pillar System

| Pillar | Share | Purpose | Best formats |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Authority** | 40–50% | Position as the expert. Share data, breakdowns, frameworks. | Text posts (hot takes, data), carousels, LinkedIn articles |
| **Personal Narrative** | 30–40% | Build trust. Share the person behind the expertise. | Text posts (stories, confessions, behind-the-scenes) |
| **Community** | 20–30% | Engage your audience. Invite their experience. | Polls, questions, comment-driven posts, spotlights |
| *(Product/Offer)* | *(10–15% max)* | *Sell, but rarely. Trust first.* | *Soft offer in text, case study carousels* |

**Why this ratio matters:**
- Too much Authority with no Narrative = you sound like a textbook
- Too much Narrative with no Authority = likable but not credible
- Too much Product = sales account — people unfollow
- Community posts are the comment magnet — include at least one per week

## Hook Patterns

Everything before "see more" (first ~210 characters). This is the entire game.

| Formula | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **F1 Platform Risk Anaphora** | "[Platform/tool] is [thing]. [Platform] is [thing]. [Platform] will [thing]." | Repetition as rhythm — builds urgency about a shift |
| **F2 R.I.P. Obituary** | "RIP [era/thing]. It was good while it lasted." | Declares an era over. Works for industry pivots |
| **F3 Year-over-Year Pivot** | "[Year] me vs. [Year] me:" | Identity contrast. High relatability for career journeys |
| **F4 Time-Anchor Confession** | "[X months/years] ago, I [believed/did] [wrong thing]." | Vulnerability + wisdom. Highest trust-builder |
| **F5 Self-Proving Meta** | "I'm going to [commitment]. Here's why:" | Proof by declaring and doing it publicly |
| **F7 Odd-Precision Money Ledger** | "We spent $[specific number] on [thing]. Here's exactly what happened:" | Precise numbers signal authenticity |
| **F8 Paid-vs-Free Reversal** | "You don't need [expensive thing]. Here's the free version:" | High shareability — people love a cheaper alternative |
| **F9 Curiosity-Gap Teaser** | "Something weird happened when we [did X]." | Open loop. Forces "see more" click |
| **F10 Contrarian + Historical Receipts** | "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's the evidence from [time period]:" | Opinion + proof. High comment potential |

**Hook rules:**
- The first line must work as a complete thought — assume 90% of readers never click "see more"
- No hedging ("I might be wrong but...", "This is just my opinion...")
- No preamble ("Today I wanted to share...", "Excited to announce...")
- No em dashes (—) — they're the #1 AI-text signal on LinkedIn
- Commit to the claim. Qualify it later in the body, not in the hook

## The Comment Strategy — The Real Growth Engine

Like X's reply strategy, LinkedIn comment engagement is the hidden growth lever that most creators ignore.

**How it works:** When you comment on someone else's post, your comment appears to their audience. A sharp, substantive comment under a high-performing post from a larger account can drive 50–200 new followers in a single day. Comments on LinkedIn are indexed and visible for longer than on most platforms.

**Comment targets by archetype:**

| Target | Goal | Comment type |
|---|---|---|
| **Peers** (similar size) | Mutual amplification | Add a new angle, extend the conversation |
| **Aspirationals** (10x bigger) | Get noticed by their audience | Sharp, specific insight — not agreement |
| **Prospects** (your target audience) | Attract followers | Answer their question or problem publicly |
| **Commenters on your posts** | Retain and deepen engagement | Reply to every comment within 2 hours |

**Daily target:** 10–20 substantive comments per day on posting days. This is not optional for growth — commenting drives more inbound than posting for most accounts under 5k followers.

**Comment quality rules:**
- Never comment "Great post! 🔥" — it's invisible noise
- Every comment should be able to stand alone as a LinkedIn post in miniature
- Add a data point, a contradiction, a personal example, or a question
- Length: 2–4 sentences. Long enough to be substantive. Short enough to read.
- The best comments get 100+ likes and convert to followers

## Posting Cadence

| Activity | Optimal |
|---|---|
| Original posts per week | 3–5 |
| Comments on others per day (posting days) | 10–20 |
| Polls per week | Max 1 |
| Newsletter issues | Weekly or bi-weekly |

**Why 3–5 posts/week max:** LinkedIn's algorithm shows your posts to roughly the same pool of followers. Posting 7 days/week means each post gets a smaller share of that pool. 3–5 high-quality posts earn more total reach than 7 mediocre ones.

**Best posting days (B2B audience, ET):**

| Day | Why |
|---|---|
| Tuesday | Best overall. Decision-makers are in work mode. |
| Wednesday | Second best. Mid-week attention peak. |
| Thursday | Strong, especially for authority posts. |
| Monday | Works for early openers. Avoid for controversial takes. |
| Friday | Reach drops 20–30% after 12 PM. |
| Saturday/Sunday | B2B reach cut 30–50%. Only for viral personal posts. |

**Best posting times:** 7:30–9:00 AM local time. Catches the morning scroll before the workday starts. Second window: 12:00–1:00 PM. Avoid after 5 PM on weekdays.

**Monday = comment day.** No original posts. Spend the time engaging on Sunday/Monday posts from your network. Your comments prime the algorithm for your Tuesday post.

## Profile as Landing Page

Your LinkedIn profile is a conversion page. Every new follower visits it within 24 hours of seeing your content. If it doesn't immediately answer "who is this, why should I care, what do they do," they won't follow.

**Banner image:** The most underused real estate on LinkedIn. Shows behind your profile photo. Use it to communicate your niche, brand, or current project in one visual.

**Headline (the most important field):**
```
DON'T: Job Title at Company
DO: [What you do] → [For whom] → [Outcome they get]
Example: "Data Engineering Lead → helping data teams ship faster with less complexity"
```

**About section (first 3 lines are visible without clicking "see more"):**
- Line 1: Who you help and how (the value proposition)
- Line 2: One credibility signal (role, company, outcome, years)
- Line 3: What you're currently building or sharing

**Featured section:** Pin your best-performing post, your LinkedIn newsletter, or a lead magnet. This is your CTA shelf — the one place on LinkedIn where you can direct people to a specific thing.

**Creator Mode:** Turn it on. Moves the Follow button above Connect, enabling follower growth from people who don't want to connect directly. Also unlocks LinkedIn Newsletter and link in profile for eligible accounts.

## Conversion Tactics

LinkedIn converts differently than other platforms. The audience is in professional mode. Overt selling triggers immediate distrust. Lead with value, then guide to the offer.

**CTA patterns that work:**
- "The full breakdown is in the first comment (no link in post — algo penalty)"
- "DM me [specific word] and I'll send you [specific resource]"
- "Save this for when you're [in the relevant situation]"
- "We solve this at [Company]. First comment has the link if you want to explore."

**CTA patterns that don't work:**
- ❌ "Check out my product!" (no context, no trust)
- ❌ "Link in bio" (LinkedIn doesn't have a bio link the way Instagram does)
- ❌ "Sign up now" (too transactional for the relationship-first culture of LinkedIn)
- ❌ "Limited time offer" (nobody believes it)

**The DM funnel:** Great post → interested commenter → you DM them (or they DM you) → conversation → offer. LinkedIn DMs have a 70%+ open rate because they're not treated like email. Prioritize DM conversations over public sales.

**Soft offer formula:**
```
[Teach something genuinely valuable]
[Show it working with a specific result]
[One-line mention of your product/service as the mechanism]
[CTA in first comment, not post body]
```
Example: "We cut pipeline costs by 60% by switching from [A] to [B]. [Two paragraphs explaining how.] We now help other teams do the same. Details in the first comment."

## LinkedIn-Specific Features

**LinkedIn Events:** Create an event for a live workshop, AMA, or product demo. LinkedIn promotes events to your followers. High-intent attendees = high-quality leads.

**LinkedIn Live:** Live video with real-time comments. Requires an application (Creator Mode + following threshold). Builds community faster than any other format — live interaction is irreplaceable.

**Polls → newsletter pipeline:** Run a poll on a topic. In the follow-up post, share the result and direct people to subscribe to your newsletter for the full analysis. Converts casual followers into newsletter subscribers (higher intent).

**Profile link (Creator Mode):** With Creator Mode, you can add one external link to your profile. Use it for your newsletter subscribe page, landing page, or portfolio — not your company homepage.

## Creator Collaboration on LinkedIn

LinkedIn doesn't have Instagram Collab posts, but collaboration still works.

**Co-written posts:** Two creators write a carousel or article together. Both post it (separately) to their audiences on the same day or week. Coordinated campaigns without a formal partnership feature.

**Guest features:** Mention a peer in your post ("I learned this from [name]") and tag them. They see the notification, often comment, which exposes you to their audience. The tag is only valuable if the mention is genuine — forced tags are easy to spot.

**Interview-style posts:** "I asked [person] one question: [question]. Their answer changed how I think about [topic]:" — pull quotes from a conversation, tag the person, get their audience in the comments.

**Mutual comment amplification:** Agree with 3–5 peers to comment on each other's posts within the first 30 minutes. The early comment boost is the highest-leverage time to have activity. Keep comments genuine — quality over speed.

## Analytics

**Track weekly:**

| Metric | Healthy benchmark |
|---|---|
| Impressions (week-over-week) | >10% growth in growth phase |
| Engagement rate (engagements / impressions) | >3% |
| Comment rate (comments / impressions) | >0.5% |
| Follower growth (week) | >100 in active growth phase |
| Profile views (week) | >200 — signals content driving curiosity |
| Newsletter subscriber growth | >50/week during active promotion |

**By format:**
- Text posts: track comment count (not likes)
- Carousels: track saves and click-through to last slide
- Polls: track vote count + follow-up comment engagement
- Newsletter: track open rate (>40% is strong for LinkedIn newsletters)

**What to do when a post performs unusually well:**
1. Note the pillar, formula, topic, and posting time
2. Repeat the same formula on a different topic within 7 days
3. Turn it into a carousel (same research, different format)
4. Turn it into a newsletter issue (expand the idea)

**What to do when engagement stalls (3+ weeks of declining reach):**
1. Check posting frequency — are you over 5 posts/week?
2. Check comment activity — are you doing 10+ comments/day?
3. Check pillar balance — is one pillar dominating?
4. Check hook quality — are your first 210 characters actually stopping the scroll?
5. Try a new format (if you've been doing text-only, try a carousel)

## Quick Win Tactics

1. **Optimize the "See more" cutoff.** Rewrite the first 210 characters of your last 5 posts. If they don't create tension or curiosity on their own, rewrite them. This single change often doubles engagement.

2. **Comment before you post.** On posting days, spend 15–20 minutes commenting on trending posts in your niche before publishing your own content. Your profile gets visits from those threads, priming the algorithm for your post.

3. **Reply to every comment within 2 hours.** This is the single highest-leverage action on LinkedIn. The algorithm counts your replies to comments as new engagement events — extending the post's reach window.

4. **Repurpose your best post as a carousel.** A text post that performed well already proved the topic works. Turn the same insight into a 7-slide carousel. Different format, same research, often higher saves.

5. **Pin your best-performing post to Featured.** Best post → pin it. Profile visitors see it immediately. Refresh every 60–90 days with your most current best work.

6. **DM everyone who comments on your high-performing posts.** Don't pitch. Just say: "Thanks for your comment on [post] — it made me think of [related thing]. Happy to connect." This turns passive engagers into warm conversations.

7. **Start the LinkedIn Newsletter with your first post's topic.** Your first newsletter issue should expand the most-performed post topic into a full piece. Promotes the newsletter, extends the post's reach, rewards your existing audience.
