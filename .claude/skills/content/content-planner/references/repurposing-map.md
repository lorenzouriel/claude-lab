# Repurposing Map

How one core idea transforms into 4 platform-native pieces. The goal is not to copy-paste — it's to find the right entry point for each platform's audience and format constraints.

---

## The transformation principle

Each platform gets a different slice of the same idea:

| Platform | What it gets | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | The full argument — structured, long-form, with receipts | LinkedIn rewards depth and professional authority |
| **X** | The sharpest single line or the compressed thread | X rewards punchy claims and scannable threads |
| **Instagram** | The visual structure — slides or a scripted Reel | IG rewards visual organization and saves |
| **TikTok** | The hook and spoken delivery — raw, fast, immediate | TikTok rewards completion and authentic speech |

**Rule:** Write LinkedIn first. It forces you to fully develop the idea. Everything else is derived from it.

## Transformation templates

### 1. Data / Insight Breakdown

**Core:** You have a data point, cost breakdown, or surprising number from real work.

| Platform | Format | Entry point | Key difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | F7 Odd-Precision Money post | The specific dollar amount or metric in line 1 | Full context, receipts, implication |
| X | T2 Number Shock tweet | The number as the opening word | Just the number + 1-line implication. Link to LinkedIn in first reply. |
| Instagram | Carousel (data-driven) | Cover slide: the number as headline | Each slide = one layer of the breakdown |
| TikTok | Listicle script | H5 Research Authority hook | Fast-paced, spoken, each item 3-4 seconds |

**What NOT to do:** Post the same paragraph on all four. The number shock works on X because it's all context. On TikTok it works because it's spoken mid-action. On Instagram it works because it's structured visually. Same data, four different presentations.

---

### 2. Contrarian Take

**Core:** You disagree with a widely-held belief in your niche and have evidence.

| Platform | Format | Entry point | Key difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | F10 Contrarian + Historical Receipts | State the consensus → pivot → your claim | Full argument with steelman + 3 evidence points |
| X | T4 Hot Take → T9 Thread Bait | Bold claim in tweet 1, thread below | 5-7 tweet thread. Tweet 1 must stand alone. |
| Instagram | Reel: Opinion format | H4 Contrarian hook (spoken) + on-screen text | 30-60 sec. Steelman in sec 3-10. Argument in sec 10-45. |
| TikTok | Contrarian script (H4) | Spoken contrarian hook in sec 0-3 | Same structure as IG Reel but faster pacing, rawer delivery |

**What NOT to do:** Post the same "hot take" text everywhere. The LinkedIn version earns the take with 1000 words. The TikTok version earns it by holding the viewer for 45 seconds. These are different proof mechanisms.

---

### 3. Personal Mistake / Lesson

**Core:** You made a real mistake, it cost you something specific, you learned something non-obvious.

| Platform | Format | Entry point | Key difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | F4 Time-Anchor Confession | "N [time] ago I [specific mistake]" as line 1 | Full story arc: mistake → cost → lesson. 800-1200 chars. |
| X | T3 Confession tweet | The same opening line from LinkedIn | Just the opening line + the lesson. 240 chars. Thread only if the story needs more space. |
| Instagram | C4 Confession caption + single image | First 2 lines = the LinkedIn opening line | Caption: 200-400 chars. The image shows the aftermath or the context. |
| TikTok | Story script (H1) | Start at the moment of maximum tension | Start with the result ("I lost $X" / "The client fired us") — not the beginning |

**The confession compression rule:**
- LinkedIn = the full arc
- X = the opening line + the lesson (nothing else)
- Instagram = the opening line + the most relatable moment + the lesson
- TikTok = the tension moment → what happened → the single lesson

---

### 4. How-To / Process

**Core:** You have a step-by-step process that produces a specific result.

| Platform | Format | Entry point | Key difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | Authority text post with numbered steps | F8 Paid-vs-Free or F5 Self-Proving Meta opener | Full process with each step explained. 1000-1400 chars. |
| X | T10 How-I thread | "How I [result] in [timeframe]:" as tweet 1 | Each tweet = one step. 5-7 tweets. No step > 200 chars. |
| Instagram | Carousel: step-by-step format | Cover: "How to [result] in [N steps]" | Each slide = one step. Headline + 30-50 word body per slide. |
| TikTok | Tutorial script | H2 Pattern Interrupt or H5 Research Authority | 30-60 sec. On-screen step labels + spoken explanation. |

**The step compression rule:**
- LinkedIn: each step gets a full sentence + context
- X: each step gets one tweet (140-180 chars max)
- Instagram: each step gets a slide headline (5-7 words) + one short paragraph
- TikTok: each step gets 5-8 seconds of screen time

---

### 5. Strong Opinion / Thought Leadership

**Core:** You have a view on where your industry is going, what's overrated, or what most people are getting wrong.

| Platform | Format | Entry point | Key difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | F5 Self-Proving Meta or F2 R.I.P. | The bold claim in line 1 | Full argument. Vulnerability moment mid-post. Data or personal receipt. |
| X | T9 Thread Bait | "I've [verb]ed [topic] for [N years]. Here's what I think is true:" | Thread: claim → steelman → evidence → reframe → open question |
| Instagram | Reel: Opinion format | Spoken contrarian hook sec 0-3 | 45-60 sec. Commit to the view. No hedging. |
| TikTok | Opinion script (H4) | Same as IG but faster, rawer | 30-45 sec. End on the strongest line, not a CTA. |

## What changes vs what stays constant

### Stays constant across platforms
- The core claim (don't dilute it per platform)
- The specific number or named entity (the most credible detail)
- The lesson or takeaway
- Your voice's point of view

### Changes per platform
- The hook mechanism (question vs number vs story opening vs spoken action)
- The length and compression level
- The format (text / slides / spoken video)
- The CTA (LinkedIn → comment; X → reply; IG → save; TikTok → comment or follow)
- The tone (LinkedIn: professional-personal; X: punchy-direct; IG: warm-visual; TikTok: raw-spoken)

---

## Sequencing: what to write first

Always write in this order:

1. **LinkedIn long-form** — forces full idea development. This is the source document.
2. **X thread** — compress the LinkedIn post into tweet-sized chunks. Tweet 1 = the LinkedIn hook.
3. **Instagram carousel/Reel** — take the structure from X but visualize it. Slide 1 = tweet 1.
4. **TikTok script** — take the Instagram Reel structure but rewrite for spoken delivery. The hook comes from the same H-formula bank.

If you only have time for one platform, write LinkedIn. The other three are derived from it and can be written in 20 minutes each once the full piece exists.
