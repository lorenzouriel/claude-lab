# Identity

> Visual reference for all generated content. Skills read this before creating visuals.

## Colors

### Primary (Core Identity)
- **Main stroke:** `#5F5F5F`
- **Stroke variation:** `#606060`, `#626262`

### Light Backgrounds
- **Pure white:** `#FFFFFF`
- **Soft background:** `#F5F5F5`
- **Slightly darker gray:** `#EFEFEF`

### Dark Mode / Inverted
- **Background:** `#000000`
- **Near-black (softened):** `#1A1A1A`
- **Light gray stroke (dark mode):** `#BFBFBF`, `#CFCFCF`

### Usage pattern
- Light mode: white/light gray backgrounds, dark gray strokes and text
- Dark mode: black/near-black backgrounds, light gray strokes
- No bright accent colors — monochromatic identity, depth through tone

## Typography

| Font | Role | Where |
|------|------|--------|
| **Fraunces** (serif) | Display & titles | Name, page headings, project/article titles, lede paragraph |
| **Inter** (sans-serif) | Body text | Paragraphs, descriptions, nav links, definition lists |
| **JetBrains Mono** (monospace) | Metadata & labels | Section labels, detail block keys, reading times, footer, CTAs |

## What never to do

- No bright or warm accent colors — brand is monochromatic
- No emoji in design elements
- No corporate stock imagery or generic tech visuals
- Never mix PT-BR and English in the same piece
- No AI-looking generated text without running through `/humanizer`
