# X Algorithm Heuristics (2026)

Verified posting rules for the X algorithm as of 2025-2026.

---

## Format rules

### No external links in the tweet body
The X algorithm deprioritizes tweets with URLs by an estimated 40-60%. Always post the link as the **first reply** to your own tweet. Pin that reply or note "link in first reply" at the end of the tweet.

### Hashtags
- 0 hashtags: best for reach (algo treats heavy hashtag use as spam signal)
- 1 hashtag: neutral, sometimes helps topic discovery for niche audiences
- 2+ hashtags: penalized. Never use more than 1.

### Character length
- **Single tweets:** 71-100 chars tend to outperform 280-char walls for engagement rate
- **Thread tweets:** 140-200 chars per card — enough substance, not a wall
- Short + punchy > exhaustive. If you need space, write a thread.

### Media
- **Video** (under 2 min 20 sec): highest reach multiplier, especially native uploads
- **Image**: ~40% impression boost vs text-only; no reach penalty
- **GIF/meme**: works for humor/reaction content; can hurt professional credibility
- **Poll**: engagement magnet but algo has deprioritized polls somewhat in 2025

## Engagement mechanics

### What boosts reach (in order of impact)
1. **Replies** — highest signal. A tweet with 20 replies beats one with 200 likes.
2. **Quote tweets with commentary** — people adding their take = strong reach signal
3. **Bookmarks** — save signal = content-worth-returning-to
4. **Retweets** — distribution signal, lower weight than replies
5. **Likes** — weakest signal; large like count with few replies = low-value content

### The first-hour window
X shows new tweets to a small seed audience first. If that seed engages (replies especially), the algo expands reach. If not, the tweet dies.

**Implications:**
- Post when your core audience is online
- Engage with early replies immediately — it signals the conversation is active
- Don't post 3 tweets in a row — space them 30-60 min apart to avoid cannibalization

### Reply boosting
Replying to replies on your own tweet keeps the conversation alive and signals to the algo that your tweet is worth surfacing to more people. **Do this within the first 2 hours.**

---

## Content rules

### Provoke a response, not just agreement
Tweets that provoke a short reply ("this", "+1", or a pushback) outperform tweets that just get likes. Write tweets with a slight edge or an open question embedded.

### One idea per tweet
Multi-point tweets (1. X, 2. Y, 3. Z in a single tweet) perform poorly vs a single punchy claim. If you have 3 points, write 3 tweets or a thread.

### Burstiness
Vary sentence length aggressively. Short sentences. Then a longer sentence that gives the reader room to breathe and understand what you actually mean. Then short again.

## Posting cadence

| Account type | Optimal cadence |
|---|---|
| Growth / personal brand | 3-5 tweets/day + 10-20 replies to others |
| Thought leader / expert | 1-2 tweets/day + heavy reply engagement |
| Brand account | 1-2 tweets/day max |

**Over-posting penalty:** 8+ tweets/day from one account → algo treats as spam. Space it out.

**Reply volume:** Replying to 10-20 tweets from peers/bigger accounts per day drives more inbound than posting more. This is the most under-used growth lever on X.
